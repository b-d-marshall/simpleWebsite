My Four Ethical Values

    Integrity

    Being honest and doing the right thing even when no one is watching.

    “Integrity” comes from the Latin word “integritas,” which means “wholeness” or “completeness.” This is derived from the Latin roots “integer” meaning “whole” or “untouched,” and “-itas,” a suffix denoting a state or condition.

    Integrity exudes a sense of trustworthiness and that one follows through with their beliefs.

    Neglecting integrity can cause an air of suspicion and loss of trust from peers, in addition to making work harder for them.

    Respect

    Treating others fairly and valuing their perspectives.

    “Respect” comes from the Latin word “respectus,” which means “regard” or “consideration.” This is derived from the Latin roots “re-” meaning “back” or “again,” and “specere,” meaning “to look.”

    This strengthens the relationship one has with their peers and makes them feel like an important part of the group.

    Being disrespectful promotes bitterness and infighting, and can make team members prefer to work alone.

    Growth

    In psychology, the process of developing physically, mentally, or spiritually.

    1550s, "stage in growing," from grow + -th, on model of health, stealth, etc. Compare Old Norse groði, from groa "to grow." Meaning "that which has grown" is from 1570s; "process of growing" is from 1580s.

    Growth is what encourages learning, even in the workforce, and allows people to develop new skills and improve ones they already have.

    Stagnating makes one fall behind their peers and even the industry in terms of ability, and can halt both creativity and innovation.

    Accountability

    Being answerable for my decisions and learning from mistakes.

    “Accountability” shares its etymological roots with “accounting”: the Latin word accomptare means “to account”; in turn this derives from computare, “to calculate,” and putare, “to reckon.”

    This shows that one is willing to learn from their words and actions "own up" to a situation when it goes different from what was expected.

    If one is not accountable, then issues (technical and moral) will become less controllable and may damage a company's reputation.
